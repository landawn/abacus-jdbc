API Design Review — CallableQuery.java
MODEL=opus-4.8  DATE=2026-06-27  RE-VERIFIED (round 2) 2026-06-27 against current committed source
(every finding re-checked against CURRENT CallableQuery.java + NamedQuery.java; line numbers refreshed; STATUS-prefixed)

1. OVERALL ASSESSMENT
CallableQuery extends AbstractQuery<CallableStatement, CallableQuery> and inherits the positional
setXxx + terminal-op API; its own additions are (a) a named setXxx(String,...) family that mirrors
NamedQuery, (b) the registerOutParameter family (index/name x int-sqlType/SQLType x scale/typeName), and
(c) the *AndGetOutParameters terminal ops. Round-2 finds the named family materially CLOSER to NamedQuery
than round 1: the SQLType setObject overloads, the abacus-Type setObject overload, and the widened
setParameters(Object,Collection) have all been implemented since the prior pass (see ALREADY-FIXED items
below). KEY MIRROR DISTINCTION re-confirmed this pass: CallableQuery's named setters delegate parameter
NAMES directly to the driver (e.g. setObject -> `cstmt.setObject(parameterName, value)` at 1457; setNString
-> `cstmt.setNString(name,value)` at 735) and therefore do NOT perform a name->index lookup and do NOT throw
IllegalArgumentException for an unknown name — so their `throws SQLException`-only clauses are CORRECT, not
an inconsistency (contrast NamedQuery, which resolves name->index and throws IAE). The one named setter that
declares IAE (setObject(String,T,Type<T>) at 1578) does so only for a null Type arg (checkArgNotNull at
1579), which is legitimate and consistent. Remaining findings are the two parity gaps (single-arg bean
setParameters, setNString CharSequence) and the multi-result-set BiResultExtractor-only ops. The
drain-correctness concern from earlier passes remains RESOLVED.
