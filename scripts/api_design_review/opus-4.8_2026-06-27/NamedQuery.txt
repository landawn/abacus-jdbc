API Design Review — NamedQuery.java
MODEL=opus-4.8  DATE=2026-06-27  (verified against source by orchestrator)

1. OVERALL ASSESSMENT
NamedQuery extends AbstractQuery<PreparedStatement, NamedQuery> and inherits the entire positional
setXxx(int,...) + terminal-op API; its own contribution is the named-parameter setter family
setXxx(String,...), which is broad and overwhelmingly consistent: every setter returns NamedQuery for
chaining, every method puts the parameter name first, boxed-primitive overloads uniformly translate
null into setNull(name, <correct Types>) (including the Float->REAL fix), and the multi-occurrence
binding ladder is identical across all setters. Coverage is actually richer than CallableQuery's mirror
(NamedQuery adds setNString(CharSequence), setRef, setArray, setObject(...SQLType), setObject(...Type<T>)).
The setObject, setParameters, and addBatchParameters families are healthy. Findings are limited to a
small throws-clause inconsistency, a couple of low-impact gaps vs the positional family, and one
documented strict-vs-lenient asymmetry.

2. NAMING ISSUES
- None actionable. setBigIntegerAsString(String, BigInteger) (1086) is verbose but accurate and mirrors
  the positional/Callable families.

3. RETURN-TYPE AND PARAMETER ISSUES
- Throws-clause inconsistency: setNull(String,int) | NamedQuery.java:147 and setString(String,String) |
  NamedQuery.java:1106 | Medium | VERIFIED. These two declare only `throws SQLException`, while every
  other setter in the family declares `throws IllegalArgumentException, SQLException` (e.g.
  setNull(String,int,String):212, setString(String,CharSequence):1170) — and both outliers in fact throw
  IllegalArgumentException ("Named parameter not found") and already @throws-document it. Recommend: add
  IllegalArgumentException to the declared throws of these two so signature matches the family and the
  Javadoc. (Cross-type note: CallableQuery's entire mirror declares only `throws SQLException` — pick one
  direction; see SUMMARY.)
- setParameters(Map) lenient vs setParameters(Object bean[, Collection]) strict | NamedQuery.java:3851
  vs 3924 / 4017 | Low | VERIFIED. The Map overload silently leaves named params absent from the map
  unbound; the bean overloads throw IllegalArgumentException when a named param has no matching property.
  Same family, opposite absence handling. Recommend: keep but make the asymmetry explicit in both
  Javadocs (or unify). Confirming check: confirm the split is intentional product behavior (compare
  DaoImpl bean vs map call sites).

4. MISPLACED METHODS (with target class)
- None. Named setters belong here; positional setters correctly live in AbstractQuery.
  setParameters(EntityId) (3875) is a package-private internal helper — not a public-API concern.

5. MISSING METHODS (with target class)
- Boxed-primitive "default-for-null" overloads absent from the named family |
  setBoolean/setByte/setShort/setInt/setLong/setFloat/setDouble (~274/362/450/538/680/812/902) | Low |
  The positional family provides setXxx(int, Boxed, primitiveDefaultForNull); the named family does not.
  CallableQuery's named family also omits these, so the omission is consistent and likely an intentional
  "keep the named API leaner" choice. Recommend: add for parity OR accept the omission deliberately.
- Calendar-aware date/time overloads absent | setDate/setTime/setTimestamp (~1344/1454/1587) | Low |
  Positional offers setDate(int, java.sql.Date, Calendar) etc.; the named family has no (String,...,Calendar)
  equivalent (CallableQuery also omits them -> consistent, likely intentional). Recommend: add for parity
  or accept. (Healthy, not a gap: the named family deliberately exposes only the long-length
  setAsciiStream/setBinaryStream/setCharacterStream overloads, matching CallableQuery.)

6. DEPRECATION / REMOVAL CANDIDATES (with replacement APIs)
- setInt(String, char) / setInt(String, Character) | NamedQuery.java:629, 654 | (already handled) | Both
  already @Deprecated with replacement setString(String, char/Character); mirror the positional/Callable
  families. No change.
- setLong(String, BigInteger) | NamedQuery.java:778 | Low | Converts via longValueExact() and can throw
  ArithmeticException — surprising for setLong. Documented and consistent across all three families.
  Recommend: keep, or (if intent is to discourage) deprecate in all three families simultaneously toward
  setBigDecimal/setBigIntegerAsString. (Cross-class with AbstractQuery/CallableQuery — see SUMMARY.)
