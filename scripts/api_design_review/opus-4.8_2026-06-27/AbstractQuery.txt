API Design Review — AbstractQuery.java
MODEL=opus-4.8  DATE=2026-06-27  (verified against source by orchestrator)

1. OVERALL ASSESSMENT
AbstractQuery<Stmt,This> is the fluent base for PreparedQuery/NamedQuery/CallableQuery; the bulk of
it is well-structured and consistent. The setXxx(int, primitive|wrapper|wrapper+default) family is
uniform (primitive, null-handling wrapper, and @Beta default-for-null triplets) with correct JDBC
Types mapping (including the deliberate Float->REAL fix). The terminal-op families are internally
consistent: queryForBoolean/Char/Byte/Short/Int/Long/Float/Double return OptionalXxx;
queryForString/BigInteger/BigDecimal/Date/Time/Timestamp/Bytes return Nullable;
queryForSingleValue/UniqueValue (Nullable) pair cleanly with queryForSingleNonNull/UniqueNonNull
(Optional); findFirst/findOnlyOne (Optional) mirror findFirstOrNull/findOnlyOneOrNull (null); and the
Class / RowMapper / BiRowMapper triad is offered consistently across findFirst, findOnlyOne, list,
stream, insert, batchInsert. The findings concentrate on one genuine duplicate, several
deprecation-consistency gaps, and a few capitalization-based naming hazards.

2. NAMING ISSUES
- foreach(Consumer<DisposableObjArray>) / foreach(Class, Consumer<DisposableObjArray>) vs the
  forEach(RowConsumer ...) family | AbstractQuery.java:8925, 8975 (vs forEach 8684/8726/8778/8836) |
  Medium | The two families differ ONLY by letter case (foreach vs forEach) and take different
  callback contracts; a one-character typo silently selects a different method. The Javadoc itself
  documents the hazard (8938-8941). The name exists to dodge lambda-overload ambiguity between
  Consumer<DisposableObjArray> and Jdbc.RowConsumer. Recommend: add an unambiguous name (e.g.
  forEachRow / forEachDisposable) and keep foreach only as a deprecated alias.
- settParameters(...) (double-t) vs setParameters(...) | AbstractQuery.java:3140, 3187 | Low | Same
  capitalization/typo hazard; the double-t disambiguates ParametersSetter<? super This> from
  ParametersSetter<? super Stmt>. Recommend: a clearer name (configParameters / setParametersForQuery).
  Confirming check: confirm a single setParameters name truly causes lambda-inference ambiguity (it
  does for the no-context overloads).

3. RETURN-TYPE AND PARAMETER ISSUES
- query2ResultSets / query3ResultSets accept only BiResultExtractor | AbstractQuery.java:5493, 5559 |
  Low | The single-result query(...) and queryAllResultSets(...) each offer BOTH ResultExtractor and
  BiResultExtractor variants, but the numbered multi-result-set methods offer only BiResultExtractor.
  (Note: CallableQuery's query2/query3ResultSetsAndGetOutParameters have the SAME limitation — this is
  a cross-class pattern; see SUMMARY.) Recommend: add the ResultExtractor overloads for parity, or
  document the intentional restriction.
- setParameters(String,String) exists but no setParameters(Object,Object) | AbstractQuery.java:2258
  (fixed-arity String overloads start at 2 args) vs ~2440 (Object overloads start at 3; the 2-Object
  form is commented out ~2417) | Low | A caller with two Object params cannot use setParameters(a,b)
  though the String case works. Recommend: add setParameters(Object,Object). Confirming check: verify
  it does not create overload-resolution ambiguity with setParameters(T, BiParametersSetter) (~2857);
  the more-specific BiParametersSetter overload should still win.

4. MISPLACED METHODS
- None. Every public method belongs on this fluent base; subclass-specific concerns (named params,
  out-params) are correctly absent.

5. MISSING METHODS
- setXxxForIndices family is partial | AbstractQuery.java:3227-3582 | Low | Covers
  Null/Boolean/Int/Long/Double/String/Date/Time/Timestamp/Object but omits Byte/Short/Float/BigDecimal
  (and Bytes). The family is @Beta. Recommend: complete the set or document the intentional subset.
- No primitive-array setParameters beyond int[]/long[]/String[] | AbstractQuery.java:2671, 2689, 2707 |
  Low | setParameters(double[]/boolean[]/short[]/...) are absent and won't bind via the generic
  setParameters(T[]) (primitive arrays don't match T[]). Recommend: add the remaining primitive-array
  overloads or document. Confirming check: confirm setParameters(new double[]{...}) does not compile
  against T[].

6. DEPRECATION / REMOVAL CANDIDATES (with replacement APIs)
- setBigIntegerAsString(int, BigInteger) duplicates setString(int, BigInteger) | AbstractQuery.java:1069
  (body: return setString(parameterIndex, value);) vs 1170 | High | VERIFIED byte-for-byte equivalent
  (both bind the BigInteger as a base-10 VARCHAR), each @see's the other, both @Beta. Genuine redundant
  pair. Recommend: keep the explicitly-named setBigIntegerAsString and remove the surprising
  setString(int, BigInteger) overload (a setString taking BigInteger is itself misleading).
- list(..., int maxResult) deprecation is inconsistent | deprecated: AbstractQuery.java:6614
  list(Class,int), 6679 list(RowMapper,int), 6834 list(BiRowMapper,int); NOT deprecated: 6749
  list(RowFilter,RowMapper,int), 6907 list(BiRowFilter,BiRowMapper,int) | Medium | VERIFIED. The 2-arg
  maxResult forms are @Deprecated ("limit on the DB side") yet the 3-arg maxResult forms they delegate
  to are not — the discouraged concept stays fully reachable without warning. Recommend: deprecate the
  (Filter,Mapper,int) variants too, or un-deprecate the 2-arg ones.
- Filter+mapper terminal-op deprecation is inconsistent | deprecated: AbstractQuery.java:6231/6291
  findFirst(RowFilter/BiRowFilter,...), 6416/6497 findFirstOrNull(...) ("use stream(...).first()");
  NOT deprecated: 6749/6907 list(filter,mapper,...), 8726/8836 forEach(filter,...), 8323/8372
  count(filter) | Medium | VERIFIED. If in-Java row filtering is discouraged enough to deprecate the
  findFirst filter overloads, the analogous list/forEach/count filter overloads are inconsistent in
  remaining blessed. Recommend: pick one policy and apply it across the filter overloads.
- count() deprecated while count(RowFilter)/count(BiRowFilter) are not | AbstractQuery.java:8280
  (deprecated), 8323, 8372 | Low | VERIFIED. count() is deprecated as inefficient (full client-side
  scan); the filtered forms do the same full scan but are kept (@Beta, justified by predicate logic not
  expressible in SQL). Recommend: a one-line Javadoc note on the filtered forms acknowledging the cost,
  or deprecate for consistency.
- @Beta inconsistency within queryForXxx | AbstractQuery.java:4793 queryForBigInteger (@Beta), 4827
  queryForBigDecimal (@Beta) vs non-Beta queryForString/Date/Time/Timestamp/Bytes | Low | These two are
  @Beta while their sibling Nullable-returning query-for methods are stable. Recommend: promote out of
  @Beta or document why they remain provisional.
- setLong(int, BigInteger) | AbstractQuery.java:790 | Low | Surprising signature (setLong with a
  BigInteger; throws ArithmeticException on overflow via longValueExact). Mirrored across Named/Callable.
  Recommend: keep (cross-family consistency + documentation) or deprecate in all three families together
  toward setBigDecimal/setBigIntegerAsString. Confirming check: usage frequency vs the explicit forms.
- (Already correct — no action) setInt(int, char)/setInt(int, Character) | AbstractQuery.java:631/656 |
  Already @Deprecated+@Beta with the correct replacement setString(int, char/Character).
